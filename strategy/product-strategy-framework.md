# Product Strategy Framework

A structured approach for defining product direction and aligning product development with long-term outcomes.

Product strategy answers one fundamental question:

**Where should the product go and why?**

---

# Purpose

The goal of product strategy is to ensure that product decisions are:

• aligned with long-term vision  
• focused on meaningful user outcomes  
• grounded in market opportunity  
• supported by sustainable business models  

---

---

# 1. Product Vision

The vision describes the future the product aims to create.

A strong vision:

• focuses on users  
• defines transformation  
• inspires teams  

Example structure:

For [target user]

Who struggle with [core problem]

Our product enables [core value]

By providing [unique capability]

---

# 2. Strategic Pillars

Strategic pillars represent the core areas of investment.

Examples:

• user acquisition  
• engagement and retention  
• platform expansion  
• AI capabilities  

Each pillar represents a **long-term strategic focus area**.

---

# 3. Product Initiatives

Initiatives translate strategy into actionable product investments.

Examples:

• launch AI recommendation system  
• build developer platform  
• expand into new market segment  

Each initiative should support a strategic pillar.

---

# 4. Roadmap Alignment

Roadmaps should reflect strategy.

A strong roadmap prioritizes initiatives based on:

• impact  
• effort  
• strategic importance  

---

# Strategy Evaluation

Product strategy should be evaluated using key questions:

• Does this solve a meaningful user problem?  
• Does this strengthen our competitive position?  
• Does this create long-term value?
