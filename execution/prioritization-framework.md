# Product Prioritization Framework

A structured approach for deciding which product initiatives should be built.

Product teams face more opportunities than available resources. Prioritization frameworks help teams focus on the initiatives that deliver the greatest impact.

---

# Purpose

The goal of prioritization is to:

• focus on high-impact initiatives  
• allocate resources effectively  
• maintain alignment with product strategy  

Strong prioritization enables disciplined product execution.

---

# Key Prioritization Factors

Product initiatives should be evaluated based on:

User Impact

How strongly does this solve a user problem?

Strategic Alignment

Does this support the product strategy?

Business Value

Will this drive growth, retention, or revenue?

Effort

How complex is the initiative to build?

Risk

What uncertainty exists around the initiative?

---

# Common Prioritization Models

Product teams often use structured prioritization frameworks.

### RICE Framework

Reach  
Impact  
Confidence  
Effort

### ICE Framework

Impact  
Confidence  
Ease

### Value vs Effort Matrix

High Value / Low Effort initiatives should typically be prioritized.

# Prioritization Best Practices

Effective prioritization requires:

• alignment with strategy  
• clear evaluation criteria  
• transparent decision making

Prioritization ensures product teams focus on initiatives that deliver the greatest value.
